Extreme-Temperature-Induced Illness in Maryland’s Low-Income Communities


By Maya Hand

Every year, there are extreme heat/cold-induced illness and deaths in Maryland. The Maryland Department of Health publishes a weekly “Cold-Related Illness Surveillance Report” (Nov.- March) and “Heat-Related Illness Surveillance Report” (May-Sept.). The National Consumer Law Center, a nonprofit that supports low-income and other disadvantaged people in the United States, has reported about temperature-related illnesses among people who cannot pay their utilities bills and so have their utilities shut off.

NCLC has proposed policy changes. According to NCLC, there is also data from the U.S. Energy Information Administration about the demographics of those who cannot afford utility services.
